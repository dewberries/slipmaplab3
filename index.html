Welcome to the grid, User.
This web map was inspired by all things cyberwave, Retro Sci-Fi, and the glowing universe of the Tron movies. The style uses neon colors, geometric shapes, and grid patterns to create a map that feels more like a sci-fi world than a real one. The intention was to make the map feel like you're navigating inside a digital circuitry realm. Imagine a mix of classic arcade vibes, cyberwave art, and futuristic tech. From the glowing roads to the dark background, the map design is meant to pull you into that retro-futuristic aesthetic.

This map utilized the four photos above as inspiration. I used bright neon colors like pink, green, blue, and yellow, all set on a dark background to make everything stand out. You will also see grid-like linear lines and spheres throughout the design that echo the design elements of the photos.

I added a circuitry pattern to the building layer to give it a bit of texture and tie into the overall digital theme.
The idea was to make the buildings feel like part of a computer chip, adding that extra layer of sci-fi, tech-inspired detail to the map.

Grid lines are a huge part of this style, so I made my own and used them as the water layer.
It gives the effect that the land is sitting on top of a grid, like it is some kind of retro simulation.

You will see a lot of flat grid planes in this kind of art, usually with mountains in the back.
But once the grid starts to show elevation or topography, it adds a whole new layer of depth.
I created a triangular grid pattern to the land hillshade to give it texture and make it look like the grid is reacting to the terrain.

There are a lot of bright gradient spheres in the reference photos, so I wanted to bring that into the map too.
I used that look for the city points, giving them a glowing, circular style that matches the synthy, neon vibe.
It helps the cities stand out while also tying directly back to the visual feel of the artwork.

The world view zoom level shows the underlying grid and neon green country borders. The neon colors and intensity will grow as the zoom level gets higher.

At the country level, you start to see major roads layered over the glowing borders. It feels like more data streams are being revealed in this digital world the closer you zoom in.

At the city zoom level, the dense road networks really bring out the grid and data stream aesthetic. The land color intensifies into a brighter blue color to add an ambiance of digital "aliveness". The map feels more electric and immersive here, like you are further inside the system. In this view, the large nearby lake reveals the base grid texture underneath, which adds to the effect that the land is just sitting on a digital plane.

Finally, at the street view zoom level, the mountain and building textures show up best at this scale. It starts to feel like you are seeing the inner workings of the map (like digital circuitry up close).

New York is more populated, and that shows in the map through dense data grids.
The road network is super structured and grid-based, which really amplifies the digital aesthetic.
It feels like the city is built right into the circuitry of the map.

Colorado is super mountainous, and it really shows off the texture I added to the land. I wanted the mountains to feel like they were generated inside a digital system.

Papua New Guinea shows off tons of water networks flowing from the mountains down into the ocean.
It almost looks like data streams branching out and cracking into the digital surface.
The rivers give off the vibe of glowing data flows breaking through the terrain.
